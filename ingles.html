About Us

AfroAscende is an educational project that promotes Afro-Brazilian representation, covering themes such as history, culture, religion, politics, and local identity.

Using simple language, it seeks to raise awareness, especially among young people, about the value of diversity and the fight against racism, in addition to promoting Afro-descendant artists and events, strengthening the connection between education and Afro-Brazilian culture.

Arrival of the first Slave Ship:

Marks the beginning of African slavery in Brazil, with the forced arrival of individuals torn from their lands and cultures for inhuman labor.

XVI to XIX

4 million enslaved people:

For over three centuries, Brazil received the largest number of enslaved Africans in the Americas, about 4 million people. Brutally exploited, they were fundamental to the country's construction, but their contribution was erased.

1889

Beginning of the erasure of Black history:

The history and contributions of the Black population began to be erased, with African culture minimized and stereotypes perpetuated by the official narrative.

2014

Institution of racial self-declaration:

Essential milestone for the recognition of Black identity, strengthening self-identification and the fight for public policies.
